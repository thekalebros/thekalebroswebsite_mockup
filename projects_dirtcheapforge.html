Dirt Cheap Forge

Home made burner/brick forge

Quick and dirty (HOT!) forge/burner example I threw together recently. For a first time metal worker, I typically recommend you start with a regular Propane burner assembly. However, once you've outgrown these options, here's how one might "level up" for cheap.

If I remember correctly, I spent about $50 on these componants. Perhaps i'll dig out those reciepts one of these days...

I went with 1/4" steel tube, with a reducer to 1/8" pipe for the jet.

Detail of the drilling a hole to act as our jet. I believe it was a 1/64" drill bit. I figured if I drilled it out too big, I could always drill and tap larger to accept a Mig Welder tip.

Putting it together! I ended up using JB Weld on the sides to hold the "jet" straight down the pipe.

Assembly complete! Now for the actual forge...

bricks from the local wood stove repair shop. stacked ontop of some 1/4" steel plate that was laying around my boss's (at the time) warehouse.

After some chisel work to create a burner Inlet, i tacked some steel supports to help hold it together. (not completely necassary btw, get creative!)

It seems to work!

Okay, it definitely works.

Hope you enjoyed and maybe even got some ideas for your next project!
