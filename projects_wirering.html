Wire Damascus Ring

Handmade Wire Damascus Ring

Let's make a ring!

First, we need some material, I thought some sort of damascus would be fun. After a quick look around a scrap yard I found some choice Wire Rope with my name all over it!

After cutting it into manageable lengths, I pulled apart the individual strands. Sometimes it's fun to mix and match different steels in the rope.

I ended up replacing the center strand with a solid rod of some sort. Let's see how th epattern turns out!

Next I wrap it back up and tack weld the ends to help keep it together. May as well stick a handle on while we're at it.

Finally we can put it to some heat! I start by softly tapping the strands together, then I put secure it in a vise and twist it down on itself.

Once i am satisfied, i section it into quarters and tack weld them together.

Once they are welded up to eachother, I have my material!

hew, lot's of work just for a hunk of metal eh?

Here is a cross section of the billet so far. I simply cut sections off the billet for various projects. Pictured here is a keychain as a sample

Not good enough welds for a knife, perfect for a ring. The larger "grey" areas in the pattern is the solid strand I added in. I think it came out pretty fun!

After boring a hole through the center of a section, into the fire it goes!

Using progressively larger hole punches, I widen the initial hole in the billet.

Once I have reached my desired size, it's time to clean it up.

After an excessive amount of filing, sanding, and polishing, we give it a spring temper.

Untill next time!
